The Chemistry & Biology Laboratories Remodeling Project was an elaborate collaborative effort of IGSOSANA members, other mountaineers at home and abroad, donors, Principals and teachers at the School.

Specifically, I will like to profoundly thank our senior Dr. Tosin Odumosu, who volunteered to hold the first site meetings with contractors and other stakeholders at the School, Members of IGSOSANA Building Committee – Arch. Ebong Ukor, Mr. Ayo Opakunbi, Mr. Yusuff Quazeem, Mr. Soji Adeyemi, Dr. Deji Ojetola, Dr. Gboyega Osinbowale, our General Secretary Mrs. Lawunmi Olafunmiloye, Principals Ajani and Jinadu.

I thank our Treasurer, Mr. Yinka Omole, who personally carried the Data Projectors all the way to Nigeria to meet the time deadline; IGSOSANA Vice-President Pastor Ayo Adebowale and Mrs. Tayo Thomas who represented the Branch at the Commissioning and dedication events. The technical team led by Architect Bolu, who has tirelessly been providing Pro Bono consultancy services for all projects on IGS campus – we cannot thank you enough. The CEO Tunnex Laboratory Engineering contractors, Mr. Tunji Olaleye Old Boy and Head Boy 1974) for such an outstanding professional work carried out on time and within budget – this is remarkable and praiseworthy.

Finally, our Global President, Professor (Venerable) K. Jaiyeoba for his leadership in deploying his convening power to preside over all site meetings for this project.  As you have invested your time and resources in the development of our Alma mater, we pray the good Lord  bless you abundantly.

Deo et Patriae!

Yinka Okuwoga

April 27, 2019.

Day 1 - Arrivals

The 106th Founder’s Day celebrations were another series of successful events spanning three days Friday 29 to Sunday 31 March 2019.

Day 1: Friday, 29 March 2019 

Arrivals & Interactions with Students

The day commenced with old students arriving from all walks of life at the School to meet with current students, teachers and members of the Parents’ Teachers Association (PTA). In particular, members of the 1969-73 really stormed the School in large number.

 

Day 2 - Bishop Akinyele Road

Day 2: Saturday, 30 March 2019 

Dedication of the Bishop Akinyele Road

Our Global President IGSOSA Prof. (Venerable) K. T. Jaiyeoba led members of the National Executive, old students, Principal, teachers and students on a walk to Molete the starting point of the Road leading to IGS. Lord Bishop of Ibadan South – Rt Rev. Popoola conducted the dedication ceremony. Others present were Dr. (Mrs.) Adeyemi – Chairperson IGS Senior School Governing Board, Chief Architect Fadele, Dr. Femi Adeyemi, and a host of others.

 

Day 2 - 1969-73 Class-Set

Day 2: Saturday, 30 March 2019 

Renovation Project of Ebun Alayande Block by 1969-73 Class Set

Global President Prof (Venerable) Jaiyeoba in company of other members of the National Executive commissioned and dedicated the project as part of their 50th Anniversary of the Class Set.

Obu and Fabu Nite @ School Dinning Hall.

In the evening, the gathering was treated to Obu Papa Raji with gari and Papa Raji bread at the Dinning Hall; bringing some sense of nostalgic memories.

Day 3 - Thanksgiving & Sick Bay Dedication

Day 3: Sunday, 31 March 2019 

Founder’s Day Thanksgiving Service @ the School Chapel

In no time the crowd dwarfed the capacity of the School Chapel. Eminent old students throng the School campus. These include, Ambassador Olu Sanu, Chief Architect Fadele, Pa Francis Aramide, Chief Adepoju, Bishop George Lasebikan, Past National Presidents, Professor Aworh and Venerable Oyewole amongst others. There was the Thanksgiving by the 1969-73 Class Set and dedication of Electronic equipment donated to the School Chapel.

The officiating ministers included, Old Student and former Principal Rev. Daramola, Venerable Oyewusi. Venerable Adebayo (Old Boy) was the main preacher, while Global President Venerable K. T. Jaiyeoba read the only Bible lesson.

The chapel was all adorned with the colourful blue and golden taped school blazer that has ever been the envy of many. The laying of the wreath at the Bishop Akinyele’s cenotaph followed immediately after the service.

Commissioning & Dedication of the Sick Bay Project by 1968-72 Class Set:

Venerable Oyewole and Venerable Jaiyeoba, both past and current National Presidents, carried out the unveiling of the plaques respectively. And, Bishop Lasebikan led the dedication prayers.

 

Day 3 - Chemistry & Biology Lab Project

Day 3: Sunday, 31 March 2019 

IGSOSANA Projects  (N20 Million) Chemistry and Biology Laboratories Remodeling Project [87-Student Capacity]

Bishop Lasebikan did the Commissioning and Dedication of this project. This was landmark project at the School for the year. This project, is in furtherance of IGSOSANA theme for the School, “Towards IGS Centre of Excellence in Science Education by 2023,” Leveraging Technology.”

The main component of the Project includes:

  1. New Lab Work Benches
  2. Vulcathene Laboratory sink and accessories
  3. Fume Cupboard
  4. Laboratory stools
  5. Electrical wiring in PVC conduit pipes
  6. Gas Installations
  7. Data Projectors, microphones
  8. General Building works – floor tiling, paintings, ceiling fans, etc.
  9. New Storage facilities with shelves
  10. Teacher’s Office
  11. New 5-Unit Toilet Facilities

Fellow Mountaineers & Friends,

Our Alma mater – Ibadan Grammar School, is in dire need of your support for $75,000 for the renovation and upgrading of the School’s Chemistry and Biology laboratories. Due to lack of funding over the years, these Labs have suffered systematic maintenance neglect and have not been upgraded in the last 50 years. In terms of impact assessment, it is estimated that over 3,000 pupils will benefit from these facilities in the next 10 years.

In order to prepare the next generation of mountaineers for the challenges of the twenty-first century, IGSOSANA is focusing on academic support and in particular: Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) education. We hope to achieve this through a Five-Year Rolling Plan culminating in IGS Centre of Excellence in Science Education by 2023. The upgrading of these labs are sine qua non to achieving this noble objective.

The main purpose of sending a child to school by any parent, is to enrich their knowledge and achieve their God-given potentials to the fullest – we want to make this a continued reality. The greatest investment anyone can make, is to invest in the next generation. According to Martin Luther King, Jnr – Life’s most persistent and urgent question is, ‘What are we doing for other?’ This is our chance and opportunity to do something for others and invest in future generation of scientists.

Join us in this noble cause by making your donations in Nigerian Naira or the US dollar through: www.igsosana.org. No amount is too small!

Deo et Patriae!

Dr. Yinka Okuwoga

President IGSOSANA

Washington DC
